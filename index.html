Web Designer

I try to be particularly careful in following new trends, but at the same time not abandoning the milestones of this discipline.

My guidelines when I work can be summarized as follows:

  • [LOGO DESIGN] : I always have in mind these characteristics when I draw it: simple, memorable, timeless, versatile, appropriate.
  • [GRAPHIC DESIGN] : orientation towards simplicity and usability according to the principles of flat design and the concept of less is more.
  • [TYPOGRAPHIC FORMAT] : attention to color, contrast and readability of the character.
  • [COLOR SCHEMA] : obsessed with the right palette to use.

Obviously I consider the style of an interface (when it's possible) in relation to its responsive design, this characteristic is a must to me.

I always join the esthetic with a more practicle objective: enabling tracking when is possible or requested and, if we are talking about web project I study the SEO elements, in order to be more effective over the potential audience.

I feel comfortable with the modern graphic tools, but I also like a lot old style softwares (for example Fireworkx CS6), and experimental and innovative apps.

Back-end Developer

I started writing code back in 2001.

At that time Visual Basic was at the sixth version and, in order to develop dynamic websites, we used to program in Classic ASP (Active Server Pages), JSP (Java Server Pages) and PHP.

I learned a bit of everything, winking a bit the eye at Microsoft world (please do not disgust those who consider themselves pure and hard programmers, there are merits and defects in every approach to programming by each subject, for those who instead have only one belief: 'open source', I will tell you that I love utopia when it's not my job).

I never got tired of experimenting, passing from the simplicity of lesser-known CMS to the public (like TYPO3) to a rather rigid and piloted programming idea like that of the MVC Framework (I remember with some pleasure CakePHP, in particular), that in someone it forcibly put the programmers' head in order.

From those years until now programming has changed a lot.

"Big G", together with other players having the same caliber, have rewritten roles in programming (think about NodeJS, Angular, Ruby, Go and so on), so there is still a loto know ... I'm still practicing ... I'm still evolving ...

By the way, in 2015, partly due to work necessities, partly due to personal curiosity, I have deepened Java programming.

I have worked with technology such as Spring, Jersey, besides of course the basis: beans, servlet, jsp, Ant, Maven and so on.

I keep on learning, because knowledge has no end, like my curiosity.

Data Analyst

Due to my studies (math, statistic and economic are the main subject) I have matured an analytical mind with predisposition and experience in study a large amount of data. I am able to orient myself among tons of information concerning a business system, in order to identify and show in a simple way the data useful to solve a problem or make a complex decision easier.

During my work journey I have dealt a lot with Data Mining and Data Auditing, often side by side

I master SQL language and I have worked on a lot of different databases (Mysql, SQL Server, Oracle, Postgress).

Always trying to understand the origin of data and any possible distortions about them, through the use of different technologies.

I was asked often to collect and analyze particular dataset in order to identify correlations and interpretative patterns for obtaining useful information in certain fields.

For medium dataset I am used to operate with Excel as a support tool for SQL, used well, it is still a very powerful and effective tool.

For particular analysis I work with specific tool. For example recently I had the occasion to use R, SAS and Tableau Public, this last in particular is an awsome software.

Furthermore on the developing side I had the chance to use some beautiful library such as Chart.js.

As a data analyst you have to be adaptive, capable of changing depending on the problem you are facing.

The callange now is Big Data and Machine Learning, I'm still looking for the opportunity to paly with toys like Mongo DB and the Spark Framework.

I am very interested in Data Scientist Profile: I think he is the natural evolution of the Data Analyst, as well as being the sexiest job ever.

IT-System Analyst

Part of my duties concerns the systems development life cycle (SDLC):

  • System Investigation
  • System Analysis
  • System Design
  • Programming
  • Testing
  • Implementation
  • Operation and Maintenance

Furthermore, I am supposed to identify, understand and plan for organizational and human impacts of planned systems, and ensure that new technical requirements are properly integrated with existing processes and skill sets.

I have worked side by side with Developer Teams during system developments, e.g. providing use cases, flowcharts, UML and BPMN diagrams. But also I have lead them in developing thanks to my technical skills.
